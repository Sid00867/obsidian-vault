# Reading Order (Most Efficient Path)

## Week 1–2: Foundations

### 1. Dyna — Richard Sutton (1990)

**Why:** The conceptual ancestor of modern world models.

- Paper: [Integrated Architectures for Learning, Planning and Reacting Based on Approximating Dynamic Programming](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/B9781558601413500304?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
- Shorter AAAI version: [Dyna, an Integrated Architecture for Learning, Planning and Reacting](https://papersdb.cs.ualberta.ca/~papersdb/view_publication.php?pub_id=500&utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

### 2. World Models — Ha & Schmidhuber (2018)

**Why:** The canonical modern world-model paper.

- Paper: [World Models (arXiv)](https://huggingface.co/papers/1803.10122?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

### 3. A Path Towards Autonomous Machine Intelligence — Yann LeCun

**Why:** The manifesto behind JEPA, world models, hierarchical planning, and autonomous intelligence.

- Original paper: https://openreview.net/forum?id=BZ5a1r-kVsf
- Companion overview: [Introduction to Latent Variable Energy-Based Models: A Path Towards Autonomous Machine Intelligence](https://huggingface.co/papers/2306.02572?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

### 4. I-JEPA

**Why:** Perhaps the most important representation-learning paper for your interests.

- Paper: [Self-Supervised Learning from Images with a Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (I-JEPA)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.08243?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
- Blog: [Meta I-JEPA Blog Post](https://ai.meta.com/blog/yann-lecun-ai-model-i-jepa/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---
